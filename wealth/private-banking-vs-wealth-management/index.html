Private Banking vs Wealth Management

A clear explanation of private banking, wealth management, and when each service may fit high-net-worth households.

In this guide
  1. Core difference
  2. When private banking helps
  3. When wealth management helps
  4. Questions to ask

Core difference

Private banking often focuses on banking, lending, and concierge-style financial services, while wealth management focuses on investment advice, planning, and portfolio strategy.

When private banking helps

Private banking can be useful for complex lending, securities-backed credit, cash management, and relationship-based service.

When wealth management helps

Wealth management may be more appropriate for retirement planning, estate coordination, tax strategy, charitable giving, and investment oversight.

Questions to ask

Ask about fees, fiduciary duties, minimum assets, lending terms, investment philosophy, and how advice is compensated.
