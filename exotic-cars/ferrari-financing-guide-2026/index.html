Ferrari financing basics

Ferrari buyers should compare more than a monthly payment. A strong plan considers purchase price, down payment, loan term, interest rate, insurance, service reserves, storage, and depreciation. Specialty lenders may treat exotic vehicles differently than normal auto loans.

Down payment and loan terms

A larger down payment can reduce monthly cost and improve approval odds. Shorter loan terms may reduce total interest but increase monthly payment. Longer terms can preserve cash but may increase total cost.

Total cost of ownership

A Ferrari budget should include insurance, registration, tires, annual service, battery tenders, storage, detailing, and unexpected repairs. Low mileage can protect resale value, but maintenance still matters.

Questions before buying

Ask whether the vehicle has complete service records, whether financing allows early payoff, how insurance values the car, and whether depreciation risk fits your goals.
